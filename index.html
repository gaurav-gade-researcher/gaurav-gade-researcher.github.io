// About

Bridging fundamental biology with translational impact

I am a graduate researcher specializing in cellular and disease mechanisms, with hands-on expertise in biochemistry, mass spectrometry, and molecular biology. My work spans nuclear receptor-mediated metabolic regulation, therapy resistance in colorectal cancer, and ferroptosis pathways.

My thesis project at Duke-NUS Medical School, National University of Singapore explores how nuclear receptors regulate fatty liver disease progression. Prior to this, I investigated how lipid metabolism promotes therapy resistance in colorectal cancer, and the mechanism by which LCN2 promotes ferroptosis at Tata Memorial Centre, Mumbai.

I believe in the power of rigorous experimental design to decode the molecular logic of disease — and ultimately, to inform therapy.
